SAT v. ACT

The SAT is a standardized exam used for college admissions in the United States along with the ACT. It doesn't matter which test you take. You take the exam that best fits your capabilities. What are the main differences between the SAT and the ACT?

SAT

  • Score ranges from 400-1600
  • You are given 3 hours + 50min for the optional essay
  • Subjects Tested:
    • Evidance Based Reading and Writing
    • Math
    • Essay(optional)

ACT

  • Score ranges from 1-36
  • You are given 175min + 30 min for the optional essay
  • Subjects Tested:
    • English
    • Math

    • Science
    • Essay(optional)
